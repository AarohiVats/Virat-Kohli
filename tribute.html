Virat Kohli — The Run Machine

“Self-belief and hard work will always earn you success.”

Early Life & Journey

Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi, India. From a young age, he was passionate about cricket. His father passed away when Virat was 18, but he used his grief as fuel to keep pushing forward.

He first came into spotlight when he captained India U-19 team to victory in the 2008 U-19 World Cup. That win opened doors to his international debut soon after.

He is known for his chases, intensity, and consistency. He dominated run charts year after year, often carrying the batting lineup.

Struggles & Comeback

Virat faced several form slumps—in 2014–15 and 2019–20, critics questioned his fitness, temperament, and consistency. But each time, he came back stronger.

In 2019, when India lost the World Cup semi-final, many blamed him. But he went on to score multiple centuries in 2020–22, proving his resilience. His fitness regime and mental approach helped him reinvent his game.

Personal Life & Legacy

Virat married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma in December 2017. They are parents to a daughter. He is very private but known for his fitness obsession, discipline, and philanthropic work.

Through his foundation, he supports causes for children's education and sports infrastructure. He is also vocal about mental health and often speaks to young athletes about perseverance.
